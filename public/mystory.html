My Story

Once upon a time... there were stories that were never meant to leave the nursery where they were born.

I have always been fascinated by beautiful books, stories and drawing. I grew up in a small country village, surrounded by animals that inhabit our forests and gardens. The books of Beatrix Potter, Antoon Krings, and Kenneth Grahame, in particular, fueled my imagination, giving me the desire to create my own universe.

Fifteen years ago, I moved to Zoufftgen. This is where that I created my very first book, an illustrated story for my niece, in which she was the heroine. Then my son was born, and every day, whether in the car, while cooking, or at bedtime, he tirelessly asked me for stories. They took place right where we live: in the streets of Zoufftgen, in the heart of the surrounding forest, or along the rivers where we used to walk. He listened, captivated, and for the ones he particularly liked, I promised myself I would turn them into a book so that he would keep a record of them forever, just for him.

But stories have their own will, and some decide not to stay locked away... In 2023, while I was illustrating one of my Christmas tales, St. Nicholas and the Zoufftgen Town Hall decided to distribute it to all the children in the village. The local newspaper reported the event, and the book had to be reprinted to fill new orders. The second book was published the following year, and the adventure keeps going ever since...

My work

Every time my son enjoyed a story, I wrote it down and saved it, until I had accumulated a hundred ideas. But a story only truly lives when it is told, so I hope they all manage to escape from my drawer and travel to you.

I like to draw or paint using different techniques: pencil, watercolor, felt-tip pens on windows, etc. The illustrations for my first books were created on Procreate, a digital tool that allowed me to bring my world to life while juggling my busy daily life as a working mom.

My books

To date, I have published my first two Zoufftgen tales. Each of the books in this series is an open door to the local heritage, which is an infinite source of inspiration for me. The places illustrated in these stories exist for real, and so do the characters. Don't believe me? Why not come and take a little tour and see for yourself?
