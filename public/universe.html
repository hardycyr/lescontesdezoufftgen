The world

The Tales of Zoufftgen take place in a real village. The places mentioned in the stories — a tree, a house, a path — can be seen, walked, recognised. This closeness creates a special bond between fiction and reality, and invites us to look differently at what surrounds us.

The old oak on the Vogelsang path is a real tree. For generations, villagers have walked past it without always imagining the stories it might tell.

The forester's house appears in several tales. It has stood at the edge of the forest for more than a hundred years.

The village main street has always run through Zoufftgen. It appears in several tales, linking the familiar places of everyday life.

Set apart from the village, the Vogelsang farm has been part of the landscape for generations. It appears in the tales as a landmark along the path.

The butterfly park of Zoufftgen is a real place in the village, which in turn inspires the world of the tales.
